Our Story: From Darkness to Hope

In 2019, we arrived in San Martín, a small community in the mountains of Oaxaca. Children studied by candlelight because there was no electricity. Mothers walked kilometers to communicate with their families. Young people dreamed of studying, but had no internet access.

The Moment That Changed Everything

María, a 12-year-old girl, asked us: "Teacher, why can children in the city study at night and we cannot?" That question broke our hearts and motivated us to create Connecting Mexico.

Today, three years later, San Martín has solar panels, high-speed internet, and a digital library. María now takes online classes and dreams of becoming an engineer. But there are thousands of communities like San Martín waiting for our help.

Why is your help urgent? In Mexico, 21 million people live without internet and 2.3 million without electricity. Every day that passes, more children lose educational opportunities. Every month we wait, more families fall behind in the digital economy.
